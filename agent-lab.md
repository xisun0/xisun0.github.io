This space is both practical and playful. I care deeply about what the arrival of agents may mean for the way people work: how they change the rhythm of thinking, the texture of production, and the everyday experience of getting things done. I do not come from a conventional technical background, but that is exactly why I have been trying these systems in practice, not just as a user, but also by building around them, with curiosity, seriousness, and a real sense of fun.

## Featured Project

### codex-speak

[`codex-speak`](https://github.com/xisun0/CotVis) is a terminal-side companion for Codex on macOS.

It launches or follows a real `Terminal.app` Codex session, binds that terminal tab to the corresponding backend session log, extracts the completed assistant reply, and turns it into broadcast-ready text or speech output.

Key ideas in the project include:

- bridging live terminal sessions with structured assistant outputs
- turning agent responses into speech-ready broadcasts
- building lightweight workflows for human-in-the-loop interaction with coding agents
- exploring practical interfaces for agent use in everyday research and work contexts
