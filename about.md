## How I Work

I am drawn to questions that connect formal research with real-world interpretation. In academic work, I am interested in how language, policy communication, and information environments shape market behavior. Outside conventional paper writing, I also care about how ideas travel: through media commentary, teaching materials, and increasingly through tools that make research and communication more usable.

My approach tends to be analytical but application-oriented. I like work that clarifies a problem, reduces noise, and makes complex systems easier to read, whether the object is a financial market, a policy signal, or an agent workflow. Across research, writing, and tool building, I am most interested in projects that combine rigor with usefulness.
